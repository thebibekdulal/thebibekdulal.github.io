TECHNO-ECONOMIC ASSESSMENT OF A UREA PLANT

Description

Urea, a nitrogen rich organic fertilizer is very much in demand in Nepal, 700,000 Metric Ton/year. Establishing a urea plant in Nepal provides food security, economic advancement and skilled manpower. Selection of feedstock poses a challenge but the cheapest and reliable source of Natural gas (27,132 kg/hr) is selected as raw material. Snamprogetti stripping technology for urea synthesis is the ideal choice as it involves a high NH3 to CO2 ratio in the reactor, making sure of the high conversion of carbamate to urea. Problem Statement / Objectives

Nepal is currently unable to meet its annual urea demand of 700,000 metric tons, relying heavily on costly imports. This reliance results in an annual financial outflow of USD 186 million.

  • Design of Urea plant in Nepal using Natural gas.
  • Techno-Economic Assessment of urea production from natural gas.
  • Run a thorough economic analysis to determine production costs, processing and distribution.
  • Conduct a thorough safety analysis to determine possible hazards.

My Role & Responsibilities

As a leader of the group of four, I assigned tasks for everyone and provided resources. Talking about the project, I designed storage tanks, urea reactor and an evaporator. I also contributed to material balance of urea synthesis section and conducted HAZOP analysis, prepared Material Safety Data Sheet and the economic analysis.

Process & Methodology

The project began with a detailed study on urea production, after which we selected methane as the raw material and adopted the Snamprogetti Stripping process. We prepared the Basis of Design (BOD), conducted material balances, and designed the P&ID using AutoCAD. Based on this, a HAZOP analysis was carried out, and MSDS were prepared. We also developed the plant layout, performed a feasibility study, and concluded with an economic analysis and final report documentation.

    Results & Outcomes

    This study assessed the feasibility of building a urea plant in Nepal using imported natural gas. Technically, the plant design is robust, featuring Steam Methane Reforming (SMR) for hydrogen, the Haber process for ammonia, and the Snamprogetti stripping process for urea production. The plant is designed to produce 700,000 metric tons of urea per year, consuming about 27,132 kg/hr of natural gas. However, economically, the project is not viable at present. The estimated capital cost is $842 million USD, with annual operating costs around $87.6 million USD. Selling urea below the subsidized price would lead to financial losses. The heavy reliance on imported natural gas and the scale of investment required make the project unaffordable without substantial long-term foreign aid or investment. While technically sound, the plant is unlikely to be realized in the near future without strong external support.
