Process Simulation by Aspen Plus

Ethanol-Assisted Coffee Oil Extraction and Biodiesel Production via Transesterification.

Description

This project details a revised process simulation for extracting triolein (coffee oil) from spent coffee grounds (SCG) using ethanol, with a significantly enhanced ethanol recovery system. The process utilizes 99.5% pure ethanol as a solvent flowing at 400 kg/hr and spent coffee grains at 100 kg/hr. The updated process includes two distinct ethanol recovery stages: one from the primary liquid extract and another from the solid byproduct stream to maximize solvent recycling. The simulation focuses on tracking key stream conditions, calculating the purity of the extracted triolein, evaluating the overall process yield, and determining the overall ethanol recovery. The results demonstrate effective triolein extraction and a high purity for the final triolein product, alongside efficient ethanol recovery for process sustainability. This project also details another process simulation for the conversion of coffee oil (obtained from a previous extraction process) into biodiesel, specifically methyl oleate, with glycerol as a valuable byproduct. The process involves transesterification using methanol and potassium hydroxide (KOH) as a catalyst. Key parameters of the process, including feed rates, reaction conditions, and separation steps, are presented. The report focuses on analyzing stream compositions, calculating the purity of the crude methyl oleate product, and evaluating two different yield metrics to assess the overall conversion efficiency. The simulation demonstrates a highly pure crude methyl oleate product and an efficient conversion of triolein.

Research and entire process simulation using Aspen Plus.

Process & Methodology

The project was first researched and the entire process was finalized. Then the process was divided into two simulations. One : Extraction of coffee oil (troilein) from spent coffee grains with effective ethanol recovery and Two: Biodiesel extraction from coffee oil via transesterification. The results were then documented after succesfull simulation.

Results & Outcomes

The refined Aspen Plus simulation accurately models an ethanol-assisted coffee oil extraction process with an integrated, highly efficient ethanol recovery system. The process successfully extracts triolein with an approximate 96.25% purity in the product stream. The overall process yield, calculated as total product mass relative to total raw material input, is approximately 80%. Crucially, the implementation of a two-stage ethanol recovery system results in an 89.82% overall ethanol recovery. The final coffee oil product is obtained at a rate of approximately 48.447 L/hr (0.8074 L/min). This highlights the process's strong potential for sustainable and economically viable triolein production from spent coffee grounds, which can then serve as a valuable feedstock for other applications like biodiesel synthesis. The Aspen Plus simulation also accurately models the transesterification of coffee oil into biodiesel. The process demonstrates a high conversion of triolein to methyl oleate, resulting in a CRUDEOIL product with an approximate 99.90% purity of methyl oleate. The conversion efficiency from the triolein in the coffee oil feed (Yield1) is approximately 98.42%, while the overall efficiency from the triolein in the initial spent coffee grain feed (Yield2) is about 78.74%. The final crude biodiesel product is obtained at a rate of approximately 52.043 L/hr (0.8674 L/min). These results highlight the process's strong potential for efficient production of high-purity biodiesel from coffee oil.
